THE BOY WHO CRIED WOLF


In a quaint village nestled among rolling hills, there lived a mischievous boy named Peter. Peter's job was to watch over the village's flock of sheep, a task he found dull and uninteresting. To liven up his days, Peter devised a plan to entertain himself. One sunny afternoon, while the sheep grazed peacefully, Peter suddenly cried out, "Wolf! Wolf! Help, please, there's a wolf!" Hearing his cries, the villagers dropped what they were doing and rushed to the boy's aid. However, when they arrived at the pasture, they found Peter doubled over with laughter, reveling in his prank.

The villagers were not amused. They scolded Peter for his dishonesty and warned him of the dangers of crying wolf. But the thrill of tricking the villagers was too tempting for Peter to resist. Day after day, he would cry wolf, and the villagers, out of concern for his safety, would come running, only to be met with his laughter and mockery. Then, one fateful day, a real wolf appeared on the horizon. Peter's heart raced as he watched the menacing creature approach the flock. He screamed for help, "Wolf! Wolf! Please, someone, help!" But this time, no one came. The villagers, tired of Peter's lies, assumed it was just another prank and chose to ignore his cries.

The wolf attacked the sheep with ferocity, and Peter, unable to defend himself, could only watch in horror as his flock was decimated before his eyes. In a cruel twist of fate, the wolf turned its attention to Peter, who, realizing his dire mistake, tried to flee. But it was too late. The wolf pounced, and Peter's cries for help went unanswered as he met his unfortunate end. The village mourned the loss of both Peter and the sheep, but they also learned a valuable lesson about the consequences of dishonesty and the importance of trust. Peter's deception had cost him everything, serving as a cautionary tale for generations to come.

Moral: Never lie, no matter how bad the situation is.
THE END
